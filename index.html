Mixed Martial Arts (MMA)


Mixed Martial Arts is the fastest growing sport in the world.
MMA encompasses disciplines from various martial arts and Olympic sports,
such as boxing, kick-boxing, karate, jiu-jitsu, muay thai, tae kwon do, wrestling and judo.

Rounds are five minutes in length and there are typically three rounds in a contest,
unless it’s a championship fight (or a non-title UFC main event), in which case,
the contest lasts five rounds.

A common misconception is that MMA has no rules, when in fact, it has many.
The Unified Rules of Mixed Martial Arts is the most common rule set used throughout the world.
All state athletic commissions in the United States adopt the Unified Rules to help regulate the sport.
